Accessibility

Last updated: July 1, 2026

The ILS Network is committed to making theILSnetwork.com (the "Site") usable by as many people as possible, including people with disabilities.

1. Our Approach

We aim to follow generally accepted accessibility practices in how the Site is built, including:

  • Semantic HTML structure and landmarks to support screen readers and assistive technology;
  • Descriptive labels and aria-label attributes on interactive elements, such as the navigation menu and social links;
  • Keyboard-operable navigation, including the mobile menu and contact form; and
  • Sufficient color contrast between text and background throughout the Site's design.

2. Ongoing Work

Accessibility is an ongoing effort. As the Site evolves, we will continue reviewing pages and features for usability across a range of devices, browsers, and assistive technologies.

3. Known Limitations

Despite our efforts, some parts of the Site may not yet fully meet every accessibility guideline. If you encounter a barrier, we want to know about it so we can address it.

4. Feedback

If you experience any difficulty accessing content or using any feature on the Site, please let us know by emailing support@theilsnetwork.com. Please describe the issue and the page where you encountered it so we can look into it.

5. Changes to This Statement

We may update this Accessibility Statement from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
